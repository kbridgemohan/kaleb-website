My Philosophy

"What good is a financial plan if the left hand doesn't know what the right hand is doing?"

— Kaleb Bridgemohan

Think about what happens when too many cooks are in the kitchen. Everyone has a role, everyone means well, but without a shared recipe, the meal suffers. Your financial life can look the same way: an investment advisor moving money in one direction, a tax preparer reacting to it after the fact, an insurance agent filling a separate gap. Each professional doing their part, but none of them talking to each other.

My approach is different. I believe your financial decisions, how you invest, how you earn, how you plan for the future, should all be informed by one coordinated strategy. Not a patchwork of advice from separate tables, but one unified plan with a single point of accountability.

The EA Credential

Federally authorized
to practice before the IRS

An Enrolled Agent is a tax practitioner licensed by the U.S. Department of the Treasury, with authorization to represent taxpayers before the Internal Revenue Service. The EA designation is the only federally issued tax credential, meaning it carries the same recognition in every state, for every type of tax matter.

Where many financial professionals operate in the investment space alone, holding an EA license means I'm also authorized to work directly with the IRS on your behalf, navigating notices, collections, appeals, and resolution processes as a recognized practitioner.
